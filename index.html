Design a Google Ads campaign for UrbanWear

A structured Search campaign plan for a Pakistani clothing brand: new men’s and women’s collections, major cities, young fashion shoppers, and a Rs. 5,000 monthly budget—with goals, keywords, ad copy, bidding, and budget controls.

Scenario

UrbanWear is promoting its new collection of men’s and women’s clothing through Google Ads. The company wants to increase website traffic, online sales, and brand awareness in major cities of Pakistan (for example Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Faisalabad).

The marketing team has a monthly advertising budget of Rs. 5,000 and needs to manage billing and daily spending within that cap. The audience is young customers interested in fashion and online shopping.

Your role: You are hired as an SEM specialist to create a Google Ads (Search) campaign for UrbanWear and complete the five tasks below.

1. Campaign goals

Align each goal with a measurable outcome in Google Ads.

  • Website traffic — Primary Search objective: clicks and sessions to the UrbanWear site from high-intent fashion queries; track click-through rate (CTR) and landing-page engagement.
  • Online sales — Conversion-focused goal: purchases or “add to cart” completed after an ad click; measure conversion value, cost per conversion, and return on ad spend (ROAS) once tracking (e.g. Google tag / GA4) is in place.
  • Brand awareness — Supporting goal in Search: impression share and branded plus category terms so UrbanWear appears when users compare options; can be reinforced later with Demand Gen / YouTube if budget allows.

In the Google Ads interface, you would pick the campaign objective closest to your main priority (often “Sales” or “Website traffic”) and still report on all three KPIs in columns and reports.

2. Two ad groups & keywords

Tight ad groups improve Quality Score and message match. Use phrase and exact match for control on a small budget; add negatives (e.g. “jobs”, “wholesale”, “free”) as you collect search terms.

Ad group A — Men’s urban & streetwear

Theme: Men’s new collection, casual and street style, online delivery in Pakistan.

Example keywords

  • men’s clothing online Pakistan
  • buy men’s shirts online Karachi
  • streetwear brands Pakistan
  • men’s jeans online Lahore
  • urban wear men
  • new men’s fashion collection

Ad group B — Women’s fashion & online shopping

Theme: Women’s new line, trend-led pieces, young shoppers buying online.

Example keywords

  • women’s clothing online Pakistan
  • trendy dresses online Islamabad
  • buy women’s tops online
  • affordable women’s fashion PK
  • new women’s collection 2026
  • online fashion store Pakistan

4. Bidding strategy (Rs. 5,000 / month)

Recommendation: Start with Manual CPC or Maximize clicks with a maximum CPC bid limit, paired with tight match types and a focused location list.

Justification: Rs. 5,000 per month is a modest budget; automated strategies like Target CPA or Maximize conversion value need enough conversion volume to learn. Until UrbanWear has steady weekly conversions, manual or “maximize clicks” with a cap avoids overspending on expensive generic terms and keeps control in the team’s hands. After 2–4 weeks of conversion data, you can test Maximize conversions with a modest daily budget or a Target CPA if the account meets minimum conversion thresholds.

5. Billing & daily budget

  • Daily budget rule of thumb: Divide monthly budget by average days per month: Rs. 5,000 ÷ ~30 ≈ Rs. 167 per day (set in Google Ads as the campaign daily budget). Google may spend up to roughly 2× the daily budget on high-traffic days but smooths spend over a calendar month so monthly totals tend to stay near your daily budget × 30.4.
  • Account budget cap: Use a monthly account spend limit (in account settings) as a hard backstop so total charges never exceed Rs. 5,000 if daily pacing spikes.
  • Billing: On automatic payments, Google bills when you hit a billing threshold or at the end of the month, whichever comes first—monitor the “Transactions” section and set email alerts for spend.
  • Efficiency: Pause low-performing keywords, add negatives, and raise bids only on terms that drive clicks or conversions at acceptable cost; reallocate any underspend mid-month rather than raising bids blindly.
